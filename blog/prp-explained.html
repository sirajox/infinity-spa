You may have heard of PRP — Platelet-Rich Plasma therapy — from celebrities, social media, or your aesthetician. But what exactly is it, how does it work, and is it really worth the investment? In this comprehensive guide, we explain everything you need to know about PRP therapy in Nigeria — from the science to the results.

What is PRP Therapy?

PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma) therapy is a cutting-edge regenerative treatment that harnesses your body's own healing power to rejuvenate skin, stimulate hair growth, and restore tissue. Here's the basic concept: your blood contains platelets — tiny cell fragments packed with powerful growth factors that your body uses to heal wounds and regenerate tissue.

In PRP therapy, we draw a small amount of your blood, spin it in a specialized centrifuge machine to separate and concentrate these platelets into a "platelet-rich plasma," then apply or inject this highly concentrated plasma into the target treatment area. The result is an intense, localized burst of healing and regenerative activity — stimulating collagen production, cellular renewal, hair follicle activation, and tissue repair far beyond what naturally occurs.

The PRP Process at Infinity Luxury Spa: Step by Step

  • Step 1 — Consultation: Your aesthetician reviews your concerns, health history, and goals to confirm PRP is right for you
  • Step 2 — Blood Draw: A small amount of blood (30–60ml) is drawn from your arm — similar to a standard blood test
  • Step 3 — Centrifugation: Your blood is placed in our specialized centrifuge and spun at high speed for 10–15 minutes, separating the plasma and concentrating the platelets
  • Step 4 — Preparation: A numbing cream is applied to the treatment area for your comfort
  • Step 5 — Application: The concentrated PRP is applied to your skin via micro-injections, micro-needling, or topical infusion depending on your treatment type
  • Step 6 — Recovery: Mild redness for 24–48 hours is normal. Your skin's regenerative process begins immediately

Types of PRP Treatment at Infinity Luxury Spa

PRP Facial (Vampire Facial)

The most famous PRP application — the "Vampire Facial" — combines PRP with microneedling to deliver growth factors deep into the skin. The micro-injuries from the needling create channels that allow the PRP to penetrate to the dermis, where it stimulates intense collagen production, improves skin texture, reduces pores, fades hyperpigmentation, and restores youthful radiance.

Results: Skin looks visibly refreshed within 1–2 weeks, with continued improvement over 3–6 months as new collagen forms.

PRP Hair Treatment

PRP hair treatment is one of the most effective non-surgical solutions for hair thinning and hair loss in Nigeria. The PRP is injected directly into the scalp at the level of the hair follicles, where the growth factors stimulate dormant follicles back into the active growth phase, strengthen existing hair, and improve scalp health.

Results: Most clients see reduced hair fall within 2–3 months and noticeable new hair growth by months 3–6. A course of 4–6 sessions spaced 4 weeks apart is recommended for optimal results.

PRP Body Treatments

PRP can also be applied to the body for scar improvement, stretch mark reduction, and skin rejuvenation. The growth factors in PRP stimulate the skin's repair process, gradually improving the texture and appearance of scars and stretch marks over a series of sessions.

PRP O-Shot (Intimate Rejuvenation)

The O-Shot uses PRP injected into specific intimate areas to improve sensitivity, address dryness, and enhance overall intimate wellness. It's a completely natural treatment using your own blood and has helped many of our clients restore comfort and confidence.

PRP vs Exosome Therapy: What's the Difference?

Exosome Therapy is the next evolution beyond PRP. While PRP uses your own platelet-derived growth factors, Exosome Therapy uses nano-sized vesicles derived from stem cells — which contain significantly more growth factors, signalling proteins, and regenerative compounds than PRP.

Exosome Therapy is generally considered more potent than PRP, with faster results and no requirement for a blood draw. It is particularly effective for advanced anti-aging, severe skin damage, and accelerated hair restoration. The trade-off is cost — Exosome Therapy starts from ₦200,000 per session versus ₦100,000 for PRP.

Is PRP Safe for Nigerian Skin?

PRP therapy is extremely safe for all skin types — including darker Nigerian skin tones — because it uses your own biological material. There is virtually no risk of allergic reaction, rejection, or hyperpigmentation. The most common side effects are mild redness, swelling, and bruising at the treatment site, which resolve within 24–72 hours.

At Infinity Luxury Spa, all PRP procedures are performed by certified aestheticians using sterile, single-use equipment in a medically controlled environment.

How Many PRP Sessions Do I Need?

For most skin concerns, we recommend an initial course of 3–4 sessions spaced 4 weeks apart, followed by maintenance sessions every 6–12 months. For hair restoration, 4–6 initial sessions are typically needed. Your aesthetician will design a personalized treatment plan during your consultation.
